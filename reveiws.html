Name -1984

George Orwell

Genre - Dystopian fiction

Reader's Reveiw - 1984 is a dystopian novel written by George Orwell. This book was published in 1949. It depicts an authoritarian regime that controls people through surveillance and mind control. The main character, Winston Smith, struggles with his identity and freedom in an oppressive society. Orwell highlights the dangerous effects of totalitarianism and the importance of individual freedom through this novel. "1984" is a powerful and thought-provoking novel that remains relevant and impactful today.

Name - The Metamorphosis

Author - Franz Kafka

Genre - Absurdist fiction, existentialism

Review:

"The Metamorphosis" by Franz Kafka is a surreal and unsettling exploration of alienation and identity. The story revolves around Gregor Samsa, who wakes up one morning to find himself transformed into a giant insect. Kafka's vivid portrayal delves into themes of isolation, family dynamics, and the absurdity of human existence. The novella challenges the reader to contemplate the complexities of human emotions and societal expectations through Gregor's surreal journey. Kafka's mastery lies in his ability to evoke empathy and introspection despite the fantastical premise, making "The Metamorphosis" a timeless classic in literature.
